# Contrarian Club Brand Guide

## Direction

The product should feel like a formal debate salon brought onto a live event screen: editorial, theatrical, and slightly old-world, but still fast enough for a room full of people voting on phones.

## Visual Principles

- **Stage first:** Public and presenter screens should feel dark, focused, and dramatic.
- **Paper for control:** Admin and form surfaces should use parchment tones, as if managing a printed debate program.
- **Gold as emphasis:** Gold is reserved for action, status, and hierarchy, not used as a full-page wash.
- **Sharp editorial frames:** Use thin borders, inset panels, and soft stage-like shadows. Avoid offset block shadows, soft cards, and rounded SaaS styling.
- **Serif authority:** Headlines use a classical serif stack. Utility text and codes stay in monospace.

## Palette

- Ink: `#08080d`
- Midnight: `#111821`
- Panel navy: `#1e2a35`
- Parchment: `#f3ead8`
- Paper: `#fff9ed`
- Antique gold: `#c8a24a`
- Bright gold: `#f0d36a`
- Ivory: `#fff8e8`
- Muted blue-gray: `#8090a5`
- Error wine: `#6f1d1b`

## Typography

- Display: `Georgia`, `Baskerville`, `Times New Roman`, serif.
- Interface: `Avenir Next`, `Segoe UI`, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif.
- Codes and labels: `SFMono-Regular`, `Cascadia Code`, monospace.

## Component Notes

- Buttons should use square corners and letterpress-like contrast.
- Primary actions are ink or gold depending on context.
- Presenter screens should read well from across a room: large serif titles, clear QR panel, minimal helper copy.
- Audience screens should prioritize confidence: one question, large tap targets, obvious status.
